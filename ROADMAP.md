# TrustCore Roadmap

This roadmap outlines planned milestones and goals for the TrustCore pre‑execution control platform.  Dates are tentative and subject to change based on feedback, research, and regulatory developments.  We welcome community input on priorities and use cases.

## 2026

### Q2 – Public Preview (current)

* Publish high‑level documentation and concept papers.
* Launch founding‑pilot recruitment program (see [`FOUNDING-PILOT.md`](FOUNDING-PILOT.md)).
* Release early design mock‑ups and brand assets (logo, landing page).
* Gather feedback from researchers and potential integrators.

### Q3 – SDK Alpha

* Deliver private alpha SDKs for popular agent frameworks (LangChain, CrewAI, Autogen).
* Implement basic policy engine with declarative constraints and context anchors.
* Provide CLI tooling for testing pre‑execution gates locally.
* Expand documentation with integration guides and API references.

### Q4 – Pilot Deployments

* Onboard pilot partners and support deployment in controlled environments.
* Integrate with enterprise systems (CRM, finance, document management) via connectors.
* Add supervision UI for human approval and override workflows.
* Build integrations with leading identity and policy management providers.

## 2027

### Q1 – Public SDK Release

* Open source the client libraries and integration adapters under an appropriate license.
* Release versioned API with stability guarantees for gating endpoints.
* Introduce plug‑ins for popular MLOps platforms and developer tools.
* Publish security assessments and compliance guidance for regulated industries.

### Q2 – Policy Marketplace & Custom Modules

* Launch a curated marketplace of policy templates for common scenarios (financial transfers, document filing, approval workflows).
* Provide modules for domain‑specific obligations (e.g., legal filings, healthcare records, payroll).
* Support multi‑tenant governance for organizations with complex hierarchies and sub‑entities.

### Beyond

* Continuity governance: implement dynamic validity tracking over long‑running workflows (see [`docs/controlled-context-continuity.md`](docs/controlled-context-continuity.md)).
* Advanced analytics and dashboards for oversight and compliance.
* Formal verification of policy correctness and conflict detection.
* Collaboration with standard‑setting bodies to help shape regulatory best practices for AI runtime governance.

We will update this roadmap as the project evolves.  Community feedback is invaluable—please open a discussion or contact us to share your priorities.
